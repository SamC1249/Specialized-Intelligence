## TL;DR of today's adversarial review

The "obvious" plan — scrape YouTube cooking videos, run NeMo / Cosmos
Curator on them, ship a CookGen-like dataset — is the **wrong** plan for
this project. Three reasons:

1. **Legal**: YouTube's ToS explicitly forbids scraping outside the
   Data API, and the Data API will not give you anything close to the
   volume needed for frontier video pretraining. See
   `docs/artifacts/2026-06-23-legal-considerations.md`.
2. **Frontier**: every credible 2026 world-model paper has *already* used
   YouTube + EPIC-KITCHENS + Ego4D. Re-doing that gives you zero
   differentiation. The actual frontier opening is **license-clean,
   long-horizon, multi-view, procedurally-grounded** data — almost nobody
   has bothered, because it is slow.
3. **Quality**: HowTo100M-style ASR is *known* to be misaligned and noisy
   (DenseStep2M, HowToCaption). Repeating that mistake at larger scale
   does not produce a better world model; it produces a louder one.

The wedge therefore is:

> **A federated, license-clean, procedurally-grounded cooking video
> corpus, with structured per-step state annotations, with a public
> contamination audit against every popular benchmark.**

Everything in this plan supports that wedge.

## The actual research questions we're trying to answer

R1. *How much truly legally re-usable cooking video exists on the public
    internet?* (CC-BY, CC0, public-domain, government-produced, university
    lecture archives.) Concrete output: a discovery report with counts +
    hours per source + license breakdown.

R2. *What is the right unit of supervision for a cooking world model?*
    Raw video alone (Cosmos-style), latent actions (DreamDojo-style),
    structured procedural steps (DenseStep2M-style), or symbolic world
    graphs (Ego2World-style)? We need a measurement, not an opinion.

R3. *What is the contamination risk between our corpus and the public
    benchmarks (WorldModelBench, MBench, HD-EPIC VQA, Ego2World,
    YouCook2)?* If we don't measure this, our numbers are noise.

R4. *Can we build a frontier-grade caption + step pipeline without
    proprietary models?* Open-weights only (Qwen2.5-VL, DeepSeek-R1,
    InternVideo2, Cosmos-Embed1).

R5. *What is the right adversarial probe set?* The MBench paper shows that
    "conservative" world models inflate consistency scores by avoiding the
    very challenges they're being measured on. We need trigger-conditioned
    probes specific to cooking (state changes: melting, browning, cutting,
    mixing).

## Backlog for the next several Adversarial-Agent runs

(*ordered by expected adversarial value, not by ease*)

1. **Contamination audit** (`Eval-Agent` to pick up). Compute clip-level
   embedding overlap between every public cooking benchmark and our
   candidate discovery set. Publish the matrix. Without this our pipeline
   is unfalsifiable.
2. **Federated PeerTube crawl** (`Curator-Agent`). PeerTube instances are
   the biggest under-explored source of long-form, license-clean video.
   We need a per-instance discovery DAG with a `robots.txt` checker, a
   rate-limiter, and a license normaliser. Targets: ~50 instances, expect
   ~3-8 kh of usable cooking video.
3. **Wikimedia Commons cooking deep-crawl** (`Curator-Agent`). Highest
   per-clip license cleanliness in existence. Tiny but golden.
4. **Open-recipe grounding** (`Annotate-Agent`). Pull WikiHow + Wholefoods
   + Open Food Facts recipes into a `recipes` table and align clips to
   recipe steps using an open VLM (Qwen2.5-VL) with the DenseStep2M
   alignment trick.
5. **Trigger-conditioned cooking probes** (`Eval-Agent`). Build an MBench-
   style probe set for state-change events: *did the onion brown? did
   the egg yolk break? did the dough double?*
6. **Latent-action distillation eval** (`Eval-Agent`). DreamDojo-style:
   train a latent action model on our corpus and measure how well a tiny
   policy can be conditioned on it. This is the *real* end-to-end signal.
7. **Reproducibility audit**. Pin every model checkpoint we use; emit a
   `MANIFEST` per pipeline run; sign manifests with the git SHA.
8. **Data card per shard**. Mandatory `datacard.md` per output shard
   following the Croissant / HF Datasets schema, with per-source license
   breakdown.

  Data API". Both of those tighten the funnel hard. If the principal of
